NEP 2020 – Pedagogy Reform
National Education Policy 2020 · Ministry of Education, Government of India
I
1

Shift from Rote to Conceptual Understanding

NEP 2020
⏱ 4 min

"The current education system, however, focuses primarily on rote memorization and on marks and achievements in high-stakes examinations rather than on learning and development."

NEP 2020 articulates a fundamental paradigm shift in how learning is conceived and practiced in Indian schools. The policy identifies that the "focus of the educational system will shift to learning outcomes" and away from a culture of rote memorization toward deep, conceptual understanding.

Core Articulation from NEP 2020

The Policy explicitly mandates that "the purpose of the assessment system will shift from one that primarily tests rote memorization skills to one that is more formative and encourages learning and development of students." This signals a transformative reimagining of what teaching and assessment are designed to achieve.

NEP 2020 (Section 4.6) states: "Pedagogy must evolve to make education more experiential, holistic, integrated, inquiry-driven, discovery-oriented, learner-centred, discussion-based, flexible, and, of course, enjoyable."

Key Shifts Mandated by NEP 2020

  • From transmission of information to construction of understanding
  • From passive reception to active engagement with ideas
  • From content delivery to competency development
  • From high-stakes terminal testing to continuous, formative assessment
  • From single-discipline silos to cross-disciplinary integration
Policy Reference: National Education Policy 2020, Chapter 4 – Curriculum and Pedagogy in Schools: Learning Should be Holistic, Integrated, Enjoyable, and Engaging, Para 4.6.
"All subjects will be taught with an aim to develop 21st century skills such as critical thinking, creativity, scientific temper, communication, collaboration, multilingualism, problem solving, ethics, social responsibility, and digital literacy."

Experiential Learning Vision

NEP 2020
⏱ 5 min

"Learning will be made more experiential, holistic, integrated, inquiry-driven, discovery-oriented, learner-centred, discussion-based, flexible, and enjoyable."

The National Education Policy 2020 envisions experiential learning as the foundational modality for school education. The policy describes learning through "hands-on activities, projects, field visits, games and sports, the arts and crafts, and stories."

NEP 2020 Vision Statement on Experiential Learning

Section 4.6 of NEP 2020 states: "Pedagogy must evolve to make education more experiential, holistic, integrated, inquiry-driven, discovery-oriented, learner-centred, discussion-based, flexible, and, of course, enjoyable." This evolution is not optional but is presented as a policy mandate binding all stages of school education.

Modes of Experiential Learning Recognized by NEP 2020

  • Art-integrated learning
  • Sport-integrated learning
  • Story-based learning
  • Toy-based pedagogy
  • Activity-based learning
  • Field visits and nature-based learning
  • Project-based and problem-based inquiry

Experiential Learning Cycle (Kolb) — as referenced in NEP 2020 pedagogy reform framework

Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Section 4.6 – Pedagogy; Section 4.29 – Arts Integration; Section 4.30 – Sports Integration.
3

Critical Thinking & Inquiry

NEP 2020
⏱ 4 min
"The highest goal of the education system is the full development of the individual — to think critically, to act creatively, and to serve ethically."

NEP 2020 positions critical thinking and inquiry not as supplementary pedagogical strategies but as the central outcomes of the educational enterprise. The policy envisions students who are "equipped with the knowledge, skills, values, and dispositions needed to thrive and contribute to society."

Policy Articulation on Critical Thinking

NEP 2020 (Section 4.6) identifies "critical thinking and inquiry-driven" learning as a core characteristic of the new pedagogical paradigm. The policy further mandates that "all subjects will be taught with an aim to develop 21st century skills such as critical thinking, creativity, scientific temper, communication, collaboration, multilingualism, problem solving, ethics, social responsibility, and digital literacy."

Inquiry-Driven Learning

  • Students learn through questions, not through answers given
  • Classroom discourse is driven by student curiosity and teacher facilitation
  • Discovery-based approaches replace didactic instruction
  • Scientific temper is cultivated as a habit of mind

"The aim of education will be developing good human beings capable of rational thought and action, possessing compassion and empathy, courage and resilience, scientific temper and creative imagination, with sound ethical moorings and values."

Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Chapter 4, Para 4.6; Chapter 1 – Vision for Education.
4

Holistic Development

NEP 2020
⏱ 4 min

"The aim of education will be holistic development of learners — cognitive, social, physical, emotional, and ethical."

NEP 2020 defines holistic development as one of its foundational principles. The policy articulates that the school curriculum "will aim to develop cognitive skills, social and emotional learning (SEL), ethical reasoning, digital literacy, knowledge of India, global awareness, critical thinking and problem solving, and communication."

Dimensions of Holistic Development

  • Cognitive: Knowledge construction, reasoning, analysis, synthesis
  • Social-Emotional: Empathy, collaboration, conflict resolution, resilience
  • Physical: Yoga, sports, nutrition awareness, health education
  • Ethical: Constitutional values, civic responsibility, environmental stewardship
  • Aesthetic: Arts, music, crafts, cultural appreciation
  • Vocational: Practical skills, craft traditions, hands-on work

NEP 2020 emphasizes that "the new curricular and pedagogical structure is aimed at making students love learning, and at enabling teachers to love teaching." This affective dimension is integral to the holistic vision.

Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Chapter 4, Para 4.1–4.6; Chapter 2 – Foundational Literacy and Numeracy.

Arts & Sports Integration

NEP 2020
⏱ 4 min

NEP 2020 explicitly integrates arts and sports as pedagogical tools and as independent domains of learning. The policy mandates that "art, music, crafts, sports, yoga, community service, and other such activities will be incorporated into the curriculum to help students develop these important areas."

Art-Integrated Learning

"Art-integrated learning will be used as a pedagogical tool to teach curriculum concepts in all subjects."

NEP 2020 (Section 4.29) mandates that art-integrated learning is not supplementary to academic learning but is itself a mode of knowledge construction. Students engage with concepts through visual arts, performing arts, folk traditions, and craft-making.

Sports-Integrated Learning

Section 4.30 of NEP 2020 states that "sports-integrated learning will be used as a pedagogical tool for teaching" mathematical concepts, physical sciences, social skills, and ethical principles. Sports are recognized as a domain for developing "teamwork, confidence, sportsmanship, and physical fitness."

Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Section 4.29 – Art-Integrated Learning; Section 4.30 – Sports-Integrated Learning; Section 24 – Promotion of Indian Languages, Arts, and Culture.

Multidisciplinary Learning

NEP 2020
⏱ 3 min

"Rigid separations between different subjects, streams, and curricular and extracurricular activities will be reduced."

NEP 2020 envisions a school curriculum that is "multidisciplinary" and dissolves the artificial separation between sciences, arts, humanities, and vocational fields. The policy mandates that "there will be no hard separations between arts and sciences, between curricular and extracurricular activities, between vocational and academic streams."

Cross-Curricular Connections

  • Mathematics and music through rhythm and pattern recognition
  • History and geography through narrative and spatial inquiry
  • Science and art through nature drawing and scientific illustration
  • Language and social science through oral traditions and storytelling
Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Section 4.22 – Curriculum Flexibility; Section 4.24 – Multidisciplinary Approach.

Classroom Transformation

NEP 2020
⏱ 4 min

NEP 2020 envisions a transformed classroom where the teacher is no longer "the sage on the stage" but the "guide on the side." The classroom is reconceived as a learning community where dialogue, inquiry, and collaborative knowledge construction are the primary activities.

Transformed Classroom Characteristics

  • Student voice is central to classroom discourse
  • Learning happens through doing and discussing, not only listening
  • Physical space is reorganized to enable collaboration
  • Multiple languages and modes of expression are welcomed
  • Local knowledge and community resources are integrated
"Teachers will be given more autonomy in designing their classrooms and pedagogical strategies to meet the diverse needs of their students."

The policy calls for "the transformation of classrooms into laboratories of inquiry" where students investigate real-world problems and engage in meaningful intellectual work.

Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Section 5 – Teachers; Section 4.6 – Pedagogy; Section 4.14 – Mother Tongue as Medium of Instruction.
8

Reform in Assessment

NEP 2020
⏱ 5 min

"The purpose of the assessment system will shift from one that primarily tests rote memorization skills to one that is more formative and encourages learning and development of students."

NEP 2020 devotes a significant section to the transformation of assessment practices. The policy identifies assessment as "an integral part of the learning process, not just a tool for measuring outcomes." Assessment is to serve learning, not merely certify it.

Assessment Reform Principles in NEP 2020

  • Formative assessment integrated into daily teaching-learning
  • Reduction of emphasis on high-stakes summative tests
  • Portfolio-based documentation of student learning
  • Competency-based assessment aligned to learning outcomes
  • Holistic Progress Card (HPC) capturing multiple dimensions of growth

NEP 2020 proposes that "Board examinations for Classes 10 and 12 will be redesigned to test primarily core competencies, concepts, and higher-order thinking skills" rather than the ability to recall information under pressure.

Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Section 4.37–4.44 – Rethinking Assessment for Student Development; Section 4.42 – National Assessment Centre PARAKH.

Experiential Learning Defined

NCF 2023
⏱ 5 min
"Experiential learning refers to learning through direct experience and reflection — a process by which knowledge is created through the transformation of experience."

NCF 2023 provides the most comprehensive definition and framework for experiential learning in the Indian educational context. The framework describes experiential learning as a "pedagogical approach where learners acquire knowledge and skills through direct engagement with tasks, activities, and reflective practice."

NCF 2023 Definition

NCF 2023 describes experiential learning as encompassing the following characteristics:

  • Learning is rooted in concrete experience
  • Reflection transforms experience into understanding
  • Abstraction connects understanding to broader concepts
  • Active experimentation tests new understanding in new contexts

The Experiential Learning Cycle in NCF 2023

The framework describes a four-stage cycle: "The learner undergoes an experience (concrete experience), reflects on what happened (reflective observation), draws conclusions and forms concepts (abstract conceptualisation), and then applies the learning in a new context (active experimentation)."

Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Part C – Pedagogy, Section on Experiential Learning; Chapter on Approaches to Teaching and Learning.
10

Inquiry-Based Learning

NCF 2023
⏱ 5 min

"Inquiry-based learning places the student's questions, ideas and observations at the centre of the learning experience."

NCF 2023 defines inquiry-based learning as a pedagogical approach in which "students learn by investigating questions, problems, or scenarios rather than by being given pre-packaged answers." The framework describes this as the "most natural form of learning" aligned with children's innate curiosity.

Stages of Inquiry-Based Learning

  • Wondering: Students observe phenomena and ask questions
  • Exploring: Students gather information, investigate, and experiment
  • Explaining: Students construct explanations based on evidence
  • Elaborating: Students apply understanding to new contexts
  • Evaluating: Students reflect on their own understanding and process

Five-stage Inquiry-Based Learning model as articulated in NCF 2023

Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Part C – Pedagogical Approaches; Section on Inquiry-Based Learning.
11

Activity-Based Learning

NCF 2023
⏱ 4 min

"In activity-based learning, students construct knowledge through direct physical engagement with objects, materials, and environments."

NCF 2023 identifies activity-based learning as a core pedagogical strategy across all school stages. The framework describes activities as "concrete encounters with the material world that serve as the starting point for abstraction and concept formation."

Principles of Activity-Based Learning

  • Learning begins with concrete manipulation of objects and materials
  • Activities are linked to specific conceptual learning outcomes
  • Students document and discuss their observations
  • Abstract principles emerge from concrete experience
  • Activities are designed to be intrinsically motivating

NCF 2023 specifies that activities must be "purposefully designed" to ensure that learning outcomes are achieved through the engagement, not merely as entertainment. The teacher's role is to plan, facilitate, and debrief activities to maximize learning.

Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Part C – Pedagogy; Foundational Stage Curriculum; Preparatory Stage Curriculum.
12

Project-Based Learning

NCF 2023
⏱ 5 min
"Project-based learning engages students in solving real-world problems or creating meaningful products through sustained inquiry over an extended period."

NCF 2023 describes project-based learning (PBL) as one of the most powerful vehicles for deep learning and cross-curricular integration. Projects are defined as "sustained, collaborative inquiries that result in a tangible product, presentation, or performance that demonstrates learning."

Features of Project-Based Learning per NCF 2023

  • Driving Question: A meaningful question that organizes and sustains inquiry
  • Sustained Inquiry: Extended engagement over days, weeks, or a semester
  • Authenticity: Connection to real-world problems and community contexts
  • Student Voice and Choice: Students make decisions about their projects
  • Reflection: Ongoing metacognitive engagement
  • Critique and Revision: Feedback loops and iterative improvement
  • Public Product: Learning is shared with an audience beyond the classroom

Project-Based Learning Flow as described in NCF 2023 pedagogical framework

Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Part C – Pedagogical Approaches; Section on Project-Based Learning and Cross-Curricular Integration.
13

Discussion-Based Learning

NCF 2023
⏱ 4 min

"Discussion-based learning creates a dialogic classroom where students learn to articulate, justify, and refine their thinking through conversation."

NCF 2023 identifies discussion as a central pedagogical mode through which "students learn to think by having to speak and write their ideas, explain their reasoning, listen to others' perspectives, and revise their views in light of evidence and argument."

Forms of Discussion in NCF 2023

  • Socratic Seminar: Text-based discussion exploring ideas through questioning
  • Fishbowl Discussion: Small group discusses while others observe and reflect
  • Think-Pair-Share: Individual reflection, partner dialogue, class sharing
  • Jigsaw Discussion: Students become experts on sub-topics and teach peers
  • Debate: Structured exploration of multiple perspectives on contested issues
Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Part C – Pedagogy; Chapter on Classroom Discourse and Democratic Learning.
14

Competency-Based Pedagogy

NCF 2023
⏱ 5 min
"Competency-based education shifts the focus from what is taught to what students are able to do with what they have learned."

NCF 2023 articulates a comprehensive competency-based framework organized around "Curricular Goals, Competencies, and Learning Outcomes." This framework aligns pedagogy, curriculum, and assessment around the development of clearly defined competencies at each stage.

Competency Framework Structure

  • Curricular Goals: Broad learning aims for each stage of schooling
  • Competencies: Specific knowledge, skills, and dispositions to be developed
  • Learning Outcomes: Measurable evidence of competency development
  • Indicators: Observable behaviours that demonstrate learning outcomes

NCF 2023 specifies that "the design of learning experiences must be backward mapped from desired competencies — teachers plan learning activities and assessments that will produce evidence of the targeted competency."

Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Part B – Curriculum; Part C – Pedagogy; Competency Framework for each school stage.
15

Cross-Curricular Integration

NCF 2023
⏱ 4 min

NCF 2023 articulates cross-curricular integration as essential to meaningful learning. The framework describes integration as occurring at multiple levels — within a subject, across subjects, and between curricular and co-curricular domains.

Levels of Integration

  • Conceptual Integration: Common concepts across subjects explored together
  • Thematic Integration: Themes like water, justice, or environment explored across disciplines
  • Skills Integration: Communication, research, and analytical skills developed across subjects
  • Values Integration: Constitutional values woven through all subject areas
Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Part B – Curriculum Design; Section on Integration and Cross-Curricular Themes.
16

Stage-Wise Pedagogical Approaches

NCF 2023
⏱ 5 min

"Pedagogical approaches must be differentiated by stage, recognising that learning processes evolve as children grow."

NCF 2023 provides stage-specific pedagogical guidance organized around the 5+3+3+4 structure mandated by NEP 2020.

Foundational Stage (Ages 3–8)

Play-based and activity-based learning. "The primary pedagogical approach at the Foundational Stage is play — structured play, free play, and play with objects."

Preparatory Stage (Ages 8–11)

Activity-based, inquiry-driven, and storytelling-based learning. Students move from concrete to semi-abstract modes of engagement.

Middle Stage (Ages 11–14)

Experiential learning, project-based learning, and discussion-based approaches. Introduction of "subject-specific disciplinary methods of inquiry."

Secondary Stage (Ages 14–18)

Critical inquiry, seminar-based discussion, research projects, and application of knowledge to real-world contexts. "Students are expected to engage with primary sources, conduct independent investigations, and produce original analyses."

Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Stage-Specific Curriculum Chapters; Part C – Stage-Wise Pedagogy.

Constructivist Approach

NCERT
⏱ 5 min
"The constructivist perspective holds that learning is an active process of constructing meaning rather than passively receiving it."

The NCERT National Focus Group Position Papers articulate constructivism as the foundational theoretical framework for pedagogical reform in Indian schools. The Position Paper on Teaching-Learning and Assessment states that "knowledge cannot be transmitted; it must be constructed by each learner through their engagement with ideas, objects, and other people."

Core Constructivist Principles

  • Prior knowledge is the starting point for new learning
  • Learning is a process of building on, modifying, and reconstructing understanding
  • Social interaction is central to cognitive development
  • Meaning is constructed through language and dialogue
  • Context shapes learning — situated cognition

Constructivist Framework as articulated in NCERT Position Papers

Policy Reference: NCERT Position Paper – National Focus Group on Teaching-Learning and Assessment; NCF 2005 Background Rationale; Learning Without Burden (1993).
18

Democratic Classroom

NCERT
⏱ 4 min

"The classroom must itself be a democratic community — one where students experience and practice the values of justice, equality, and freedom."

NCERT's Position Paper on Teaching-Learning and Assessment articulates the democratic classroom as both a pedagogical principle and an educational value. The paper states that "education for democracy cannot be achieved through authoritarian pedagogy; the processes of teaching and learning must themselves embody democratic values."

Characteristics of a Democratic Classroom

  • All students' voices are valued and given space
  • Rules and norms are co-constructed with students
  • Diversity of language, background, and perspective is celebrated
  • Power is shared between teacher and students
  • Disagreement and questioning are welcomed, not suppressed
  • Collaborative decision-making about learning is practised
Policy Reference: NCERT Position Paper – National Focus Group on Problems of Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe Children; NCF 2005 Chapter 2; NCERT Position Paper on Gender Issues in Education.
19

Learner-Centered Pedagogy

NCERT
⏱ 4 min
"Learner-centered pedagogy begins with where the child is, not where the curriculum wants the child to be."

NCERT Position Papers consistently articulate learner-centeredness as the defining orientation of reformed pedagogy. The papers describe learner-centered classrooms as spaces where "the needs, interests, experiences, and prior knowledge of each learner shape the design and delivery of learning."

Principles of Learner-Centered Pedagogy

  • Diagnosis of prior knowledge precedes instruction
  • Learning experiences are differentiated to meet diverse needs
  • Student interests are leveraged as entry points into the curriculum
  • Pacing and sequencing are responsive to learner progress
  • Assessment informs teaching rather than merely ranking students
Policy Reference: NCERT Position Paper – National Focus Group on Teaching-Learning and Assessment; NCF 2005; Learning Without Burden (1993).
20

Knowledge Construction

NCERT
⏱ 4 min

"Knowledge is not a fixed body of facts to be transmitted; it is a living structure that each learner must actively construct and continuously reconstruct."

NCERT Position Papers draw on Piagetian and Vygotskian frameworks to articulate knowledge construction as the central cognitive process in learning. The papers describe three modes of knowledge construction: "knowledge through direct experience with the physical world; knowledge through social interaction and language; and knowledge through reflection and abstraction."

Implications for Classroom Practice

  • Teachers must create conditions for active knowledge construction, not passive reception
  • Conceptual conflicts and cognitive dissonance are productive for learning
  • Students must have opportunities to articulate, test, and revise their understanding
  • Assessment must reveal the structure of student understanding, not just correct answers
Policy Reference: NCERT Position Paper – Teaching-Learning and Assessment; NCF 2005 Chapter 2 – Learning and Knowledge.
21

Classroom Dialogue

NCERT
⏱ 4 min
"Dialogue is not merely a pedagogical technique; it is the fundamental medium through which knowledge is constructed and values are developed."

NCERT Position Papers draw on Freirean and Vygotskian frameworks to articulate classroom dialogue as the primary mode through which learning and development occur. The papers distinguish between "the recitation script" — where teachers ask questions and students provide expected answers — and "genuine dialogue" — where questions are open, multiple answers are possible, and students think together.

Forms of Productive Classroom Dialogue

  • Open questioning that invites multiple perspectives
  • Probing and follow-up questions that deepen thinking
  • Student-to-student dialogue without teacher mediation
  • Discussion of disagreements and conflicting interpretations
  • Collective reflection on the process of learning
Policy Reference: NCERT Position Paper on Teaching-Learning and Assessment; NCF 2005 Chapter 2; Language and Learning Position Paper.
22

Reflective Practice

NCERT
⏱ 4 min

"Reflective practice is the capacity to reflect on action so as to engage in a process of continuous learning — for both teachers and students."

NCERT Position Papers articulate reflective practice as essential for both teacher professional development and student metacognition. The papers describe reflection as "the bridge between experience and learning — without deliberate reflection, experience does not automatically generate knowledge."

Reflection in Student Learning

  • Students maintain learning journals documenting their thinking
  • Exit cards capture what was learned and what remains unclear
  • Portfolio entries require students to explain the significance of their work
  • Self-assessment develops metacognitive awareness

Reflection in Teacher Practice

  • Post-lesson reflection on what worked and what needs adjustment
  • Peer observation and collegial dialogue
  • Action research into classroom problems
  • Documentation of student learning trajectories
Policy Reference: NCERT Position Paper on Teacher Education for Curriculum Renewal; NCF 2005 Chapter 5 – Teacher Education.

Assessment–Pedagogy Alignment

PARAKH
⏱ 5 min

"Assessment and pedagogy must be aligned — what is assessed must match what is taught, and how it is assessed must match how it is learned."

PARAKH (Performance Assessment, Review, and Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development) is the national assessment centre established under NEP 2020 to oversee the transformation of assessment practices across India's school system. PARAKH's core mandate is to ensure "the alignment of assessment with the pedagogical principles of NEP 2020 and NCF 2023."

Assessment-Pedagogy Alignment Framework

  • Constructive Alignment: Learning outcomes, learning activities, and assessment tasks are mutually reinforcing
  • Assessment for Learning: Assessment is used during instruction to improve learning
  • Assessment of Learning: Assessment at the end of a period to evaluate achievement
  • Assessment as Learning: Students use assessment as a tool for self-reflection and goal setting

Assessment-Pedagogy Alignment model as mandated by PARAKH / NEP 2020

Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Section 4.42 – PARAKH; PARAKH Framework Document; NCF 2023, Part D – Assessment.
24

Formative Assessment

PARAKH
⏱ 5 min
"Formative assessment is the ongoing process of gathering evidence about student learning and using that evidence to adjust teaching and learning."

PARAKH and NCF 2023 define formative assessment as "assessment that is embedded in the daily flow of teaching and learning, not separated from it." The purpose of formative assessment is "to inform teaching decisions and provide feedback to learners to help them progress."

Formative Assessment Strategies

  • Observation: Systematic teacher observation of student work and discussion
  • Questioning: Diagnostic questions to reveal student understanding
  • Exit Slips: Brief written responses at the end of a lesson
  • Portfolios: Collections of student work showing growth over time
  • Peer Assessment: Students evaluate each other's work against clear criteria
  • Self-Assessment: Students reflect on their own learning using rubrics
  • Classroom Discussion: Informal assessment through observation of participation

NCF 2023 specifies that "formative assessment must account for at least 50% of assessment across all stages" and should "provide actionable feedback that students can use to improve their performance."

Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Part D – Assessment; PARAKH Framework; NEP 2020 Section 4.37–4.44.
25

Competency-Based Questioning

PARAKH
⏱ 4 min

"Competency-based questions assess what students can do with knowledge, not merely what they know."

PARAKH has developed guidelines for competency-based question design that align with NCF 2023's Learning Outcomes framework. Competency-based questions are defined as questions that "require students to apply knowledge and skills to novel situations, analyse data or texts, evaluate arguments, or create new products."

Taxonomy of Competency-Based Questions

  • Recall: Identify, define, list — assesses foundational knowledge
  • Understanding: Explain, summarise, classify — assesses conceptual grasp
  • Application: Use, solve, demonstrate — assesses transfer to known contexts
  • Analysis: Compare, examine, differentiate — assesses analytical thinking
  • Evaluation: Justify, critique, assess — assesses judgement and reasoning
  • Creation: Design, produce, construct — assesses generative capacity
Policy Reference: PARAKH Competency-Based Assessment Framework; NCF 2023 Learning Outcomes; NEP 2020 Section 4.39.
26

Blueprint Integration

PARAKH
⏱ 4 min

PARAKH mandates that examination blueprints must be redesigned to reflect the competency-based framework of NCF 2023. The blueprint specifies "the distribution of marks, question types, cognitive levels, and content domains across an assessment instrument."

Blueprint Components per PARAKH Guidelines

  • Competency Mapping: Each question is mapped to specific learning outcomes
  • Cognitive Level Distribution: Minimum 40% questions at application level or above
  • Question Type Diversity: MCQs, short answer, long answer, case-based, and source-based questions
  • Internal Choice: Students given choice to demonstrate competency through different paths
  • Rubric Specification: Clear marking criteria aligned to competency descriptors
Policy Reference: PARAKH Assessment Blueprint Guidelines; NCF 2023 Part D; CBSE and State Board Reform Frameworks aligned to PARAKH.
27

Holistic Progress Reporting

PARAKH
⏱ 5 min
"The Holistic Progress Card represents a shift from ranking students to reporting on the full spectrum of each student's development."

NEP 2020 and PARAKH mandate the adoption of a Holistic Progress Card (HPC) to replace or supplement grade-based report cards. The HPC documents "student development across cognitive, social-emotional, physical, and ethical dimensions of growth."

HPC Components

  • Academic Competencies: Subject-wise competency attainment mapped to NCF 2023 outcomes
  • Social-Emotional Learning: Collaboration, communication, empathy, resilience
  • Physical Development: Participation in sports, yoga, and health activities
  • Creative and Aesthetic Development: Engagement with arts, music, and crafts
  • Values and Citizenship: Constitutional values, community service, environmental responsibility
  • Student Self-Assessment: Student's own reflection on their learning and growth
Policy Reference: NEP 2020 Section 4.40 – Holistic Progress Card; PARAKH HPC Framework; NCF 2023 Part D – Assessment and Reporting.
28

Teacher Role in Assessment Reform

PARAKH
⏱ 4 min

"Teachers must become skilled observers, diagnosticians, and responsive facilitators of learning — not administrators of tests."

PARAKH's framework positions the teacher as the primary agent of assessment reform. NEP 2020 and PARAKH call for teachers who are "empowered to design their own formative assessment tools aligned to the competencies they are developing in students."

Teacher Competencies for Reformed Assessment

  • Design competency-aligned learning tasks and rubrics
  • Observe and document student learning during activities and discussions
  • Provide specific, actionable, and growth-oriented feedback
  • Use assessment data to adjust instruction
  • Support student self-assessment and goal-setting
  • Maintain portfolios of student learning evidence
  • Report holistically on student development, not just grades
Policy Reference: PARAKH Framework; NEP 2020 Section 5 – Teachers; NCF 2023 Teacher Education Chapter.
29

School-Based Assessment

PARAKH
⏱ 4 min

NEP 2020 mandates a shift toward school-based assessment as the primary mode of evaluating student progress, particularly at the Foundational, Preparatory, and Middle stages. PARAKH provides guidance on designing rigorous, equitable, and pedagogically aligned school-based assessments.

Principles of School-Based Assessment

  • Assessment is embedded in the daily life of the classroom
  • Multiple modes and formats capture diverse competencies
  • Assessment criteria are transparent and co-created with students
  • Assessment is used to support learning, not merely to rank students
  • Internal assessment carries significant weight in final grading
Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Section 4.37–4.40; PARAKH Framework; NCF 2023, Part D – Assessment Design.

National Achievement Survey Alignment

PARAKH
⏱ 4 min

"PARAKH will redesign the National Achievement Survey to assess competencies as defined in NCF 2023, replacing content-knowledge-based testing."

PARAKH oversees the National Achievement Survey (NAS), which is the large-scale assessment of student learning at national level. Under the NEP 2020 framework, NAS is being redesigned to "measure the development of competencies defined in NCF 2023 rather than content recall."

NAS Reform Priorities under PARAKH

  • Questions measure higher-order thinking, not just recall
  • Data disaggregated to identify equity gaps across states and districts
  • Results used for systemic improvement, not school ranking
  • State-level surveys aligned to NAS framework for coherence
  • PARAKH coordinates with states to build assessment literacy
Policy Reference: NEP 2020 Section 4.42; PARAKH Mandate; National Achievement Survey Guidelines.

Bagless Days & Vocational Education

NEP 2020
⏱ 4 min
"Students of Grades 6–8 shall be exposed to vocational crafts such as carpentry, electric work, metal work, gardening, pottery making, etc., as decided by States and schools."

NEP 2020 mandates "bagless days" in which students engage in hands-on, practical, and community-connected learning. The policy specifies that "at least 10 'Bagless Days' per year will be devoted to vocational subjects, arts integration, community service, and field experiences."

Purposes of Bagless Days

  • Integration of vocational skills with academic learning
  • Cultivation of dignity of labour and appreciation for skilled work
  • Community visits and interaction with local craftspeople and professionals
  • Reduction of exam anxiety through joyful, low-pressure learning
  • Development of practical life skills
Policy Reference: NEP 2020, Section 4.26 – Vocational Education; Section 4.6 – Pedagogy.

Toy & Game-Based Pedagogy

NCF 2023
⏱ 4 min

"Play is the child's work — and toys and games are the tools through which children explore, understand, and represent their world."

NCF 2023 articulates toy-based pedagogy as a core pedagogical approach, particularly at the Foundational Stage. The framework states that "toys and games are not merely recreational; they are instruments of cognitive, social, emotional, and motor development when used with pedagogical intentionality."

Toy-Based Pedagogy Principles

  • Local, indigenous toys connect learning to cultural heritage
  • Puzzles and building games develop spatial and logical reasoning
  • Storytelling games develop language and narrative skills
  • Games with rules develop mathematical thinking and social cooperation
  • Art and craft materials develop fine motor skills and creative expression

Mother Tongue & Multilingual Pedagogy

NCERT
⏱ 4 min
"A child best learns in the language they think in — the language of their home and community."

NCERT Position Papers on Language Learning advocate strongly for the use of the mother tongue and home language as the primary medium of instruction, at least through the early years of school. The papers cite extensive research showing that "foundational literacy and numeracy are most effectively developed in the child's first language."

Multilingual Pedagogy Framework

  • Home language is the bridge to school language and academic learning
  • Multiple languages are resources, not barriers
  • Code-switching and translanguaging support comprehension
  • Bilingual teaching materials support students transitioning to school language
Policy Reference: NEP 2020 Section 4.11–4.14; NCERT Position Paper on Language Learning; NCF 2023 Language Policy.

Equity in Assessment

PARAKH
⏱ 4 min

"Assessment must be designed to be fair, inclusive, and culturally responsive — assessing what students know and can do, not the cultural capital they bring."

PARAKH's framework on equity in assessment recognizes that "conventional assessments have often disadvantaged students from marginalised communities by testing cultural familiarity rather than academic competence." PARAKH mandates assessment designs that are "linguistically accessible, culturally sensitive, and multiple-mode."

Principles of Equitable Assessment

  • Questions are free from cultural, linguistic, and gender bias
  • Multiple formats allow diverse learners to demonstrate competency
  • Accommodations for students with disabilities are built in
  • Assessment data is disaggregated to identify equity gaps
  • Remediation and support are triggered by assessment evidence
Policy Reference: PARAKH Equity Framework; NEP 2020 Chapter 6 – Equitable and Inclusive Education; NCF 2023 Inclusive Pedagogy.

Teacher as Facilitator of Learning

NCF 2023
⏱ 5 min
"The teacher's role must fundamentally shift from being the primary source of knowledge to being the architect of learning experiences."

NCF 2023 articulates a transformed conception of the teacher role that is aligned with the constructivist and experiential learning framework of NEP 2020. The teacher is described as "a learning designer, facilitator, mentor, and co-inquirer — someone who creates conditions for deep learning rather than delivering content."

The Teacher as Facilitator: Core Functions

  • Learning Designer: Plans rich, purposeful learning experiences aligned to competencies
  • Facilitator: Guides inquiry, manages discussion, scaffolds thinking without replacing it
  • Diagnostician: Observes learners continuously and responds to their needs
  • Mentor: Builds trusting relationships that support risk-taking and growth
  • Co-inquirer: Models curiosity, intellectual humility, and love of learning
  • Reflective Practitioner: Continuously evaluates and improves their own practice

NCF 2023 emphasises that "teacher autonomy and professional trust are prerequisites for transformed pedagogy — teachers cannot be expected to facilitate learner agency while being treated as implementers of fixed scripts."

Policy Reference: NCF 2023, Chapter on Teacher's Role; NEP 2020 Section 5; NCF 2023 Teacher Education Framework.
