    About CBL

    Competency-Based Learning

    Understanding the philosophy behind NEP 2020

    Competency-Based Learning (CBL) is an educational approach that focuses on students demonstrating mastery of specific skills and knowledge before advancing. Unlike traditional time-based education, CBL emphasizes what students can do rather than what they have been taught.

    Core Principles

    CBL is grounded in four key principles: (1) clear learning outcomes defined in terms of competencies, (2) flexible pacing allowing students to progress upon mastery, (3) varied instructional methods to suit different learners, and (4) continuous assessment embedded in daily learning rather than isolated examinations.

    NEP 2020 and CBL

    India's National Education Policy 2020 represents a landmark shift from rote learning to competency-based assessment. The policy calls for the development of 21st century skills including critical thinking, creativity, communication, and collaboration—often called the 4Cs of modern education.

    NCF 2023 Framework

    The National Curriculum Framework 2023 operationalizes NEP 2020 by defining competencies across learning areas, emphasizing experiential and inquiry-based learning, and integrating values education with academic subjects.

    PARAKH

    PARAKH (Performance Assessment, Review, and Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development) is the national body established under NEP 2020 to set standards for student assessment, develop competency-based assessment tools, and guide states in transforming their evaluation systems.

    💡 Reflection Questions

    2. Which competencies do you think are most important for your future?

    3. How can teachers assess 21st century skills in daily classroom activities?
